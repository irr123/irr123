I'm validating demand for [Backsight](https://backsight.app).

This is a live log -- 24h in. Numbers at the bottom; they didn't go where I
expected.

![lone indie hacker broadcasting a surveyor's-crosshair signal flare into a grimdark cathedral of the dead internet -- a swarm of hollow-eyed ad-bots, one human ember answering, walled "no self-promotion" gardens, Warhammer 40k style](hero.png)

Right now I'm not trying to "launch" in the Product Hunt / Hacker News sense. A
narrower question:

> Can targeted traffic convert into early-access signups?

So the funnel matters. The ideal path:

**post -> landing page -> email**

Not:

**post -> long discussion -> explanation -> maybe landing page -> maybe email**

Comments are secondary. The signal is whether the promise is strong enough for
someone to click and join the waitlist.

I listed the obvious places -- Hacker News, Product Hunt, Reddit, Indie Hackers,
quantified-self and journaling communities -- then read the rules. Most won't let
an early waitlist post stand: `Show HN` wants a usable artifact, Product Hunt
wants polish, half the subs ban self-promo or market research, `r/indiehackers`
karma-gated me out. Which channel survives moderation is the whole game -- that
table is its own post. One board took the post clean: `r/SideProject`.

## 24h later

Posted when each one was ready -- no timing optimization, no best-hour-to-post
games. So these are an un-tuned floor, not a ceiling.

| Channel                                     |          Views | Signups |
| :------------------------------------------ | -------------: | ------: |
| `r/SideProject`                             |            203 |       1 |
| X -- my own account, 60 followers           | 29 (+1 repost) |       0 |
| `r/QuantifiedSelf` weekly megathread        |              6 |       0 |
| X -- reply under an 18k account YC reposted |              7 |       0 |
| Telegram channel, 11 subs                   |              5 |       0 |
| **Total**                                   |        **250** |   **1** |

`r/SideProject` carried it: 203 of 250 views, 81%, and the only signup. One
permissive, low-friction channel beat everything else combined.

Borrowed reach is a myth. The reply under an 18k-follower account -- one Y
Combinator had reposted -- pulled 7 views. My own 60-follower post pulled 29.
Replying to a big account borrows none of its audience.

The gatekept subs were dead weight. The rule-compliant `r/QuantifiedSelf`
megathread comment: 6 views. `r/Journaling` and `r/indiehackers` never let the
post stand at all. Rule-compliant and traffic-dead turn out to be the same
outcome.

Relabel the nodes -- swap Reddit for X for Telegram -- and the shape holds:
permissive open channel >> gatekept garden >> borrowed reach. The platform names
are interchangeable; the proportions aren't. That's the part worth keeping once
the exact counts rot.

## Hypothesis

The real hypothesis is not:

> Can I get attention?

It is:

> Can the promise "your notes become stats" convert targeted visitors into
> early-access signups?

If the answer is no, more traffic will not fix the product. If yes, the next
question is which channel brings the highest-intent early users.

24h in, the honest read: 1 in 250, ~0.4%. One signup is a pulse, not validation
-- n is tiny -- but it's a real human, and the channel question is the one that
actually moved. `r/SideProject` first, big-account piggybacking never. This is
the next round, not the verdict.

## The other reply

One human signed up -- a real early-access email. The other thing that reached
my inbox, routed through Reddit, was a pitch for a tool that "brings traffic to
your startup."

I posted asking for traffic. One person answered; a marketer tried to sell me
more of it.

Dead internet theory, one inbox at a time.

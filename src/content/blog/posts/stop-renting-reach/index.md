The part that outlasts this week's numbers: stop renting reach. Build my own
place with my own rules -- this blog, a list I own, an audience that isn't one
ban away from gone.

Immediate plan, for the record:

1. Double down on `r/SideProject`; queue `r/alphaandbetausers` next.
2. Stop feeding gatekept gardens -- megathread-only, market-research-banned,
   karma-gated. The traffic isn't there.
3. Submit to early-product directories.
4. Save Hacker News and Product Hunt for later, when there is something usable.

## "My own rules" starts at the login box

Every "Sign in with Google / Meta / X / GitHub" button rents your front door to
a landlord who can change the locks. One Google ban took a man's Gmail, Photos,
and Google Fi number in a single shot -- the police couldn't even phone to tell
him he'd been cleared; his number was locked inside the dead account.[^sso]
Appeal is a form, not a person.

It's why Backsight is accountless: identity is your keys, not someone's OAuth.
